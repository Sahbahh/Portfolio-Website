Anomaly Detection

Spring 2023

Tools:

This group project is from Cybersecurity CMPT318 course at Simon Fraser University. In this project, my team explored the application of various machine learning techniques, specifically Hidden Markov Models (HMM) and Reinforcement Learning (RL), for the purpose of anomaly detection in household electricity consumption data over a three-year period.
The team started by preprocessing the time-series dataset using Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to reduce dimensionality, selecting the most informative features while minimizing redundancy. This step allowed for better visualization and understanding of the dataset and the relationships between its components.
Once the data was prepared, we trained and tested a multivariate Hidden Markov Model (HMM).Our team faced challenges in determining the right number of states, initially leading to overfitting and then underfitting the model. However, through trial and error, the model was adjusted to achieve better performance.
As an alternate approach, reinforcement learning was discussed due to its potential advantages in the context of the project. RL employs an agent, which learns to make optimal decisions based on reward and penalty mechanisms derived from its interactions with the environment. Given the sequential nature of time-series data and the possibility of incomplete data, RL offered flexibility in handling these conditions, thereby improving the robustness of anomaly detection.
RWe measured the results based on the Log Likelihoods and Bayesian Information Criterion (BIC) of the HMM model with different datasets. The datasets with injected anomalies showed that the model was capable of detecting these irregularities.
Through the course of the project, we learned valuable lessons about PCA, HMM, and RL, their applications in time series analysis, and the importance of careful feature selection and state determination. We also acknowledged the benefits of troubleshooting, testing, and analysis in reaching optimal solutions. Overall, this project serves as an exploration of advanced machine learning methodologies for effective anomaly detection in time series data.
